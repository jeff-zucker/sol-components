sol-feed

Fetches RSS or Atom news feeds and shows the articles on your page. Point it at one feed, or at a list of feeds grouped by topic, and pick how it looks with the view attribute: a single feed, a topic picker, or a grid that mixes articles from every feed in a topic.

Try it

Use it in your HTML

Add this line once, near the top of your page — it makes the component available:

<script src="dist/sol-loader.min.js" data-bundles="sol-feed"></script>

Then drop the tag wherever you want the feed to appear:

<sol-feed source="https://example.org/rss.xml"></sol-feed>

Options

AttributeWhat it does
view How it looks. feed (the default) shows one feed as a list of article links. topic shows a picker of feed sources for one topic, with the chosen source's articles below it. all shows a grid of article cards mixed from every feed in a topic, with buttons to switch sources.
default-selected For view="all" only. A comma- or pipe-separated list of feed names (or bits of their web address) to tick on the visitor's first visit, e.g. "guardian" matches "The Guardian". After that, the visitor's own choices are remembered.

Some attributes here are shared across components — see Common attributes. For source: with view="feed" it's a feed's web address; with topic and all it points at a settings file plus a #Topic on the end, e.g. data/feeds.ttl#News. Most feeds can't be read directly by a browser, so set proxy to a CORS proxy address that the component reads the feed through.

Examples

One feed, shown as a list of links:

<sol-feed
    view="feed"
    source="https://example.org/rss.xml"
    proxy="http://localhost:3002/proxy?uri="
></sol-feed>

A topic picker — point source at a small settings file and a #Topic inside it:

<sol-feed
    view="topic"
    source="data/feeds.ttl#News"
    proxy="http://localhost:3002/proxy?uri="
></sol-feed>

A mixed grid of every feed under a topic:

<sol-feed
    view="all"
    source="data/feeds.ttl#Feeds"
    proxy="http://localhost:3002/proxy?uri="
></sol-feed>

The settings file lists your feeds, grouped into topics. A small one in Turtle looks like this:

@prefix ui: <http://www.w3.org/ns/ui#> .
@prefix bk: <http://www.w3.org/2002/01/bookmark#> .

<#Feeds> a bk:Topic ; ui:label "Feeds" .
<#News>  a bk:Topic ; ui:label "News" ; bk:subTopicOf <#Feeds> .

:00012 a ui:Link ;
  ui:label    "NY Times" ;
  bk:recalls  <https://rss.nytimes.com/services/xml/rss/nyt/World.xml> ;
  bk:hasTopic <#News> .

The #News on the end of source picks which topic to show. The same list can also be written in SKOS — a ready-made twin lives at data/feeds-skos.ttl.
